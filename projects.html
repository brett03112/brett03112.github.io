Data Analysis of Primes using Various Number Bases with Information Theory

A computational research project that applies information theory concepts to analyze prime numbers across different numerical base representations (decimal, binary, octal, and hexadecimal). Using metrics like Shannon Entropy, Mutual Information, and Markov chain modeling, the project uncovers non-conventional patterns in prime numbers. Key findings include varying entropy characteristics across different bases, identification of rare digit transitions, optimal segmentation lengths for each base, and strong cross-base relationships. The methodologies developed can be applied to other domains including cryptography, genomics, natural language processing, financial market analysis, and quantum computing.
